Case Study: Utah Votes

Adulting is hard, voting shouldnâ€™t be. This case study is based on real world problem and shows the process we used to come up with the solution. How can we better serve voter participation in Utah while understanding and working with the motivating factors influencing these actions?

Overview

In 2016 the baby boomers & other generations are now the minority voters, as the millennials & GenX population is greater. How can we help Millennials and GenX voters engage in the process of voting?

Solution

Daily expectations at work and home are busy and complex for most adults. Often times those obligations leave little time for civic awareness and knowledge about the things and people impacting their communities. Voters want to be engaged, but the amount of information and time involved is daunting.

The solution is to create a way to:

  • Have the easiest and quickest access to unbiased information about voting issues
  • Spend less time both in the process of learning AND voting
  • Be an informed voter

Results

This product offers real solutions to the problem at hand by assisting to connect, inform & educate voters. Utah Votes has potential to become a useful tool & could inspire voters that need clear digestible information. This solution was designed as apart of a bootcamp.
