Utah Votes

ABOUT

Adulting is hard, voting shouldn’t be. This case study is based on real world problem and shows the process we used to come up with the solution. How can we better serve voter participation in Utah while understanding and working with the motivating factors influencing these actions?

How can we help Millennials and GenX voters engage in the process of voting?

META

This project was done during a UX Bootcamp. Through this open-ended prompt, I was able to hone my skills to focus on creating a solution that most accurately addressed the main researched problem.

Role

UX Design, Research, UI design

Tasks

Start with research and solve for the main problem at hand

Time

4 weeks

Overview

Challenge

In 2016 the baby boomers & other generations are now the minority voters, as the millennials & GenX population is greater. How can we help Millennials and GenX voters engage in the process of voting?

Solution

Daily expectations at work and home are busy and complex for most adults. Often times those obligations leave little time for civic awareness and knowledge about the things and people impacting their communities. Voters want to be engaged, but the amount of information and time involved is daunting.

The solution is to create a way to:

  • Have the easiest and quickest access to unbiased information about voting issues
  • Spend less time both in the process of learning AND voting
  • Be an informed voter

Results

This product offers real solutions to the problem at hand by assisting to connect, inform & educate voters. Utah Votes has potential to become a useful tool & could inspire voters that need clear digestible information. This solution was designed as apart of a bootcamp.

Process

The process of this project took me on the following journey

Surveys

First survey & assumptions

Prior to starting the project I listed the assumptions concerning the brief. These assumptions shaped a survey crafted and then conducted to over 70 Utah participants within our specified target audience. Here were a few main key points of the consensus from the first survey:

Learned key points

  • Voting takes too long (People have good intensions but in the end, work family, etc. supersede the best of intentions)
  • People only vote when it is convenient
  • People want an easier option then mail-in ballots and voting in-person (i.e. secure technology)

Second survey

The results of the first survey help my team and I to realize we had more questions to ask to get the details and to the root of the problem more clearly.

We conducted a second survey with questions that were a bit more refined. This survey proved more useful in guiding our process and helping us craft a clear solution. Data was collected from 137 participants.

Takeaways

  • The majority of people felt it is their civic duty to vote, so in most cases they will to vote.
  • People only want to spend 1-2 hours voting

Learned key points

Non-voters largely felt that two things could fix their lack of voting problem

  • Access to unbiased, clear information
  • Secure voting technology

Persona

Persona based on the research done

We developed a persona based on the survey and summarization done thus far. Base on the data and the person we developed we concluded that voters goals are the following:

  • Have the easiest and quickest access to unbiased information about voting issues
  • Spend less time both in the process of learning AND voting
  • Be an informed voter

Story Mapping

Ideation for solutions

Initially we spent a lot of time considering a electronic method of secure voting technology. We knew that saving time was one of our guiding principles. We also knew that non-voters cited that a key solution would be to securely vote electronically. However, after realizing the many barriers to potentially solve that particular solution we decided to re-visit our data. We were reminded of one thing: The majority of people interviewed actually DID vote. So how could we help solve their other pain points? We chose to focus our solution on education and time.

  • Have the easiest and quickest access to unbiased information about voting issues
  • Spend less time both in the process of learning AND voting
  • Be an informed voter

Story mapping

After a few versions and collaboration on the team our site architecture came to life. Our story mapping showed the journey of how to educate, inform & connect our voters.

  • Create an easy way to research elections, candidates & ballot issues, based on your location and interests.
  • Make it clean and fun in order to appeal to the young voters and straightforward enough for anyone to use.
  • Provide a motivating solution

User testing

Testing the desgin

We conducted 5 guerrilla user tests on the first prototype. After conducting a few of these tests we determined that a significant problem was the main CTA was clear enough for users to navigate. More then half of our initial user group failed to click on what we wanted them to take action on first.

"I could not find any text or links indicating voting issues, so I thought that the precinct info might eventually lead me to a link with voting issues for each candidate"
"It is the only area I see with voting information, I assume it would bring up a bigger list"

Design & Iterate

What would be next?

I would test the Challenge feature, Do people understand how to participate in voting challenges? Can people find value with this type of learning? Can they complete challenges and compete with their peers? How do you gamify this type of learning better?

Another test that would be valuable would be to test providing a sample ballot to help people vote easier.‍

Last, I would test the depth of interest, how far does a voter need to learn in order to vote? Can we curate their challenges based on their interests as well? In general, testing would be next steps, lots of testing and iteration.

Retrospective

Ask the right questions upfront

Retrospective

Through the process of finding solutions to this problem, I realized the value of asking the RIGHT questions. Spending more time on this stage would have been more valuable to saved us time in the long run - giving us more useful information to analyze and interpret.

I also realized the importance of adaptability and being open minded in solution finding. Continuously revisiting your persona guided our work and helped us pivot numerous times, a large pivot towards solving perhaps the largest complaint our surveys suggested.

The solutions we developed was one answer to the problem, but going in that direction was based on continuously revisiting our quantitative and qualitative data. I felt that we solved for the largest paint point, and the end result seemed to be a viable solution that with the right team and finessing could make a major difference in local communities.
